Overview

Combining the efforts of computational and experimental researchers, the goal of this Center is development of an integrated suite of validated computational tools that are designed to accelerate the development of new structural metals.

Our Center is formally known as the DOE Software Innovation Center for Integrated Multi-Scale Modeling of Structural Metals. We call it PRISMS for short which stands for PRedictive Integrated Structural Materials Science. Our ambitious goal is for our PRISMS tools to be the extensible scientific core of the Integrated Computational Materials Engineering.

The goals of our Center are:

  1. Establish an Integrated Multi-Scale Modeling Framework and Open Source Software (PRISMS)
  2. Develop Advanced Open Source Computational Methods
  3. Tightly Couple Experiments and Models
  4. Application and Validation – PRISMS Demonstrator: Magnesium, Fatigue & Ductility
  5. Establish the Materials Commons: An Open Source Knowledge Repository and Virtual Collaboration Platform for the PRISMS Community

Funding

The Center is funded by a five year $11M grant from Department of Energy Basic Energy Science. This has been coupled with $1.5M in cost-shared from UM, College of Engineering and the faculty and departments involved. The funding comes from the Materials Genome Initiative, President Obama’s plan to double the speed with which American scientists and engineers discover, develop, and manufacture new materials.

Center Computing Resources

The PRISMS Center has a dedicated 2072 core cluster for running our models on. The cluster will be shared by our different research groups as they develop our advanced open source computational models.
